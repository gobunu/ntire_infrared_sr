Challenge Overview

This track is part of the NTIRE 2026 Workshop & Challenges co-located with CVPR 2026 in Denver, and it invites the community to develop robust infrared (IR) remote sensing super-resolution methods.

Why IR SR is tricky

Infrared remote sensing images often have limited spatial resolution due to sensor hardware constraints. That can hide small objects, fine textures, and weak thermal anomalies — hurting downstream tasks like detection and change analysis.

Thermal credibility matters

Unlike visible-light images, IR intensities are tied to thermal radiation and can behave nonlinearly. SR outputs should not only look clear, but also preserve reliable thermal consistency and meaningful temperature contrasts across land-cover types.

Data & Competition Phases

Where to get the data

All datasets are hosted on the competition platform under the Files section.

Development: Training

Only high-resolution (HR) images are provided. Participants are encouraged to design their own degradation model to synthesize low-resolution (LR) inputs for training.

Development: Validation

Validation inputs are released (LR only) to support model debugging and comparison.

Test Phase

Final test inputs (LR only) are released. Teams submit reconstructed SR outputs and provide reproducible code or an executable for final evaluation.

Evaluation

Metrics

The primary metrics are PSNR and SSIM. The final score is:

Score = PSNR + 20 × SSIM

Tip: optimizing “visual sharpness” alone can be deceptive in IR. Try to preserve thermal patterns and contrast that downstream applications depend on.

Submission requirements

  • Submit SR results for the test set by the result deadline.
  • Submit a Fact Sheet and reproducible code/executable by the code deadline.
  • Top teams will be invited to present at the NTIRE workshop.
